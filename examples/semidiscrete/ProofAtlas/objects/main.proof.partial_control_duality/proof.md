Consider the subspace $\mathcal{U}$ of $L^1_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;L^2(\Omega;E_j))$ generated by observed traces of solutions to [[main.model.backward_adjoint_system]]:

$$
\mathcal{U}\triangleq
\left\{
\chi_{G_0}z
\mid
z \text{ solves the backward adjoint system}
\text{ with }
\eta\in L^2_{\mathcal{F}_T}(\Omega;E_j)
\right\}.
$$

Define a linear operator $\mathcal{L}$ on $\mathcal{U}$ by

$$
\mathcal{L}(\chi_{G_0}z)
=
-\mathbb{E}\int_{\mathcal{M}}y_0 z(0).
$$

By [[main.claim.observability]],

$$
|\mathcal{L}(\chi_{G_0}z)|
\leq
\left(
\frac{C_4 e^{C_4 2^j+\tau T}}{T^2}
|y_0|^2_{L^2_{\mathcal{F}_0}(\Omega;L^2_h(\mathcal{M}))}
\right)^{1/2}
|\chi_{G_0}z|_{L^1_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;L^2(\Omega;E_j))}.
$$

Thus $\mathcal{L}$ is bounded on $\mathcal{U}$. By the Hahn-Banach theorem, it extends to a bounded linear functional on

$$
L^1_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;L^2(\Omega;E_j)).
$$

By the Riesz representation theorem, cited as Theorem 2.55 of [[source.lue_zhang_2021]], there exists

$$
u\in L^\infty_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;L^2(\Omega;E_j)).
$$

This proves [[main.construction.partial_control_duality]].
