Partial differential equations are one of the fundamental tools for describing various physical phenomena in the natural world and constitute an important area of mathematical research.

Due to the complexity of real-world systems, many phenomena in fields such as chemistry, biology, microelectronics, pharmaceuticals, communications, and transmission require the use of stochastic partial differential equations for more accurate modeling, and their controllability problems have been extensively studied. In particular, for the null controllability of stochastic parabolic equations with only one control, related works include [[source.lue_2011]], [[source.yang_2016a]], and [[source.liu_2014a]].

As a natural approximation of control systems, it is reasonable to consider semi-discrete systems. That is, whether the controllability of the continuous problem is preserved under discrete numerical approximation, meaning that as the mesh size tends to zero, the continuous properties are recovered; see [[source.zuazua_2005]]. Due to the well-known counterexamples in two dimensions cited through [[source.zuazua_2005]] and [[source.lecaros_2025]], new phenomena may arise at the discrete level, leading to the failure of uniform null controllability in the general sense. Therefore, the study of controllability for semi-discrete systems is more intricate.

The controllability of deterministic semi-discrete parabolic equations has been widely investigated; see, for example, [[source.boyer_2010]] and [[source.boyer_2010a]]. However, only a few works have addressed the controllability of semi-discrete stochastic parabolic equations. In particular, [[source.zhao_2024]] established the null controllability for linear semi-discrete stochastic parabolic equations, and recently [[source.lecaros_2025]] studied the controllability of high-dimensional stochastic semi-discrete parabolic equations, but with two controls acting on both the drift and diffusion terms.

The main contribution of this paper is to prove the $\phi$-null controllability for semi-discrete parabolic systems in arbitrary dimensions, under certain assumptions, with only one control on the drift.
