Let $T>0$ and let $(\Omega,\mathcal{F},\mathbf{F},\mathbb{P})$, with $\mathbf{F}=\{\mathcal{F}_{t}\}_{t \geq 0}$, be a complete filtered probability space on which a one-dimensional standard Brownian motion $\{W(t)\}_{t \geq 0}$ is defined.

Let $\mathbf{F}$ be the natural filtration generated by $W(\cdot)$, augmented by all the $\mathbb{P}$ null sets in $\mathcal{F}$. Write $\mathbb{F}$ for the progressive $\sigma$-field with respect to $\mathbf{F}$.

Let $\mathcal{H}$ be a Banach space. The paper uses the following stochastic function spaces:

- $L^2_{\mathcal{F}_\tau}(\Omega;\mathcal{H})$ denotes the space of all $\mathcal{F}_\tau$-measurable $\mathcal{H}$-valued random variables $\xi$ such that $\mathbb{E}|\xi|^2_{\mathcal{H}}<\infty$.
- $L^2_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;\mathcal{H})$ is the Banach space of all $\mathcal{H}$-valued, $\mathbf{F}$-adapted processes $X(\cdot)$ with $\mathbb{E}\big[|X|^2_{L^2(0,T;\mathcal{H})}\big]<\infty$.
- $L^\infty_{\mathbb{F}}(0,T;\mathcal{H})$ consists of all essentially bounded, $\mathcal{H}$-valued, $\mathbf{F}$-adapted processes.
- $L^2_{\mathbb{F}}(\Omega;C([0,T];\mathcal{H}))$ denotes the Banach space of all $\mathcal{H}$-valued, $\mathbf{F}$-adapted continuous processes $X(\cdot)$ such that $\mathbb{E}\big[|X|^2_{C([0,T];\mathcal{H})}\big]<\infty$.

All controls and stochastic solutions in the Atlas objects are interpreted with respect to this filtration and these adapted/progressive spaces.
